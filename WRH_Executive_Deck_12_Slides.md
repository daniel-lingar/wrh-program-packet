# WRH Executive Deck (12 Slides)
*Short. Sharp. Declarative. Every slide is a single, powerful idea.*

---

## Slide 1 — What Really Happened
People are not mysteries.  
Patterns are not accidents.  
Lives do not collapse out of nowhere.  

WRH teaches one core truth:  
> “What looks like failure is often survival.”

WRH gives people a map of what happened, what their body learned, what pattern formed, and what becomes possible next.

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem: People Are Judged at the End of the Pattern
Most systems only see the aftermath:
- the reaction
- the relapse
- the shutdown
- the explosion
- the avoidance
- the collapse

People are judged at the moment their alarm is already firing.

WRH asks:  
> “What happened before this happened?”

---

## Slide 3 — The Survival Map
The Survival Map explains where the pattern came from. It has five parts:
1. **The Water**
2. **The Alarm**
3. **The Survival Moves**
4. **The Rescue Boat**
5. **The Way Back**

The Survival Map gives people language for what they’ve only felt as confusion.

---

## Slide 4 — The Water
The Water is the conditions a person lived inside before the pattern formed. Not the event — the environment.
- The atmosphere.
- The pressure.
- The instability.
- The fear.
- The loneliness.
- The silence.

The Water teaches the body what danger is.

---

## Slide 5 — The Alarm
The Alarm is the body’s danger system.
- It reacts before the mind can explain.
- It fires based on resemblance, not logic.

The Alarm is not weakness. It is protection. But a protector trained in old danger can create new damage.

---

## Slide 6 — The Survival Moves
The Survival Moves are what the person does when the alarm fires.
- Fight / Flight / Freeze / Fawn
- Numb / Disappear / Perform
- Explode / Shut down

These moves are not character flaws. They are adaptations. They once worked. Now they may be costing the person everything.

---

## Slide 7 — The Rescue Boat
The Rescue Boat is what the person grabs when the survival moves are not enough. It kept them afloat — not free.
- Substances / Work / Control
- Isolation / Fantasy / Food
- Sex / Chaos / Another person

The Rescue Boat helped them survive something they could not survive alone. Now it may be keeping them trapped.

---

## Slide 8 — The Way Back
The Way Back is what becomes possible when the whole pattern is visible. It is not a miracle or instant transformation.
- It is structure, repetition, and support.
- It is replacement and direction.

One pause. One interrupt. One safer support. One moment where the old pattern does not get the final word.

---

## Slide 9 — The Trajectory Engine
The Trajectory Engine explains how direction changes. It is the mechanical relationship between:
**pattern → fork → interrupt → repetition → evidence → direction**

Trajectory is not fate. It is not destiny. It is what happens when the interrupt repeats long enough to build a new path.

---

## Slide 10 — Why WRH Works
WRH works because it is:
- non‑clinical / non‑punitive
- mechanical, not mystical
- repeatable / teachable / scalable

WRH gives people:
- a map of their pattern, choices, consequences, and direction.

It replaces shame with sequence and pressure with possibility.

---

## Slide 11 — Best‑Fit Settings
WRH is designed for high‑barrier, high‑pressure environments:
- reentry, veteran, and recovery programs
- peer‑support and diversion programs
- youth resilience, shelters, and rural outreach
- workforce‑readiness programs

WRH gives these systems a shared language for change.

---

## Slide 12 — Implementation / Next Step
WRH can be implemented as:
- a full curriculum or modular training
- a staff‑training or participant‑facing program
- a trauma‑literacy foundation
- a decision‑making and accountability tool

### Next steps:
1. Identify program goals
2. Select WRH modules
3. Train staff in the Survival Map + Trajectory Engine
4. Launch participant groups
5. Begin evidence tracking

Build direction, one repeatable move at a time.
