# WRH 24‑Slide Expanded Training Deck
*Built for training, pitching, onboarding, and deep explanation.*

---

## Slide 1 — Title Slide
**What Really Happened**  
A trauma‑literacy framework for understanding patterns, direction, and change.

---

## Slide 2 — The WRH Premise
People do not change because they are told to change.  
People change when they can see the pattern they are running, understand where it leads, interrupt it once, repeat the interrupt, and narrate a new direction.

---

## Slide 3 — The Problem
Most systems judge people at the end of the pattern:
- the reaction / the relapse
- the shutdown / the explosion
- the avoidance / the collapse

WRH asks:  
> “What happened before this happened?”

---

## Slide 4 — Why This Matters
When systems only see the aftermath, they miss:
- the conditions
- the alarm
- the survival move
- the rescue attempt
- the pattern underneath

WRH replaces judgment with sequence.

---

## Slide 5 — The Survival Map
The Survival Map explains where the pattern came from. It has five parts:
1. **The Water**
2. **The Alarm**
3. **The Survival Moves**
4. **The Rescue Boat**
5. **The Way Back**

---

## Slide 6 — Why the Map Comes First
A person cannot interrupt a pattern they cannot see.  
The Survival Map gives language to what people have only felt as confusion.

---

## Slide 7 — The Water
The Water is the conditions a person lived inside before the pattern formed. Not the event — the environment.
- The pressure / The instability
- The fear / The loneliness
- The silence

The Water teaches the body what danger is.

---

## Slide 8 — What the Water Teaches
The Water trains the system to:
- scan / brace / hide
- perform / disappear
- predict danger

These are not flaws. They are adaptations.

---

## Slide 9 — The Alarm
The Alarm is the body’s danger system.
- It reacts before the mind can explain.
- It fires based on resemblance, not logic.

The Alarm is not weakness — it is protection.

---

## Slide 10 — How the Alarm Works
The Alarm asks one question:  
> “Have I seen this before?”

If yes, the body prepares:  
- fight / flight / freeze / fawn / numb / disappear

---

## Slide 11 — The Survival Moves
The Survival Moves are what the person does when the alarm fires.
- They are automatic.
- They are patterned.
- They once worked.

Now they may be costing the person everything.

---

## Slide 12 — Examples of Survival Moves
- shutting down / exploding / avoiding
- overexplaining / disappearing / numbing
- performing / controlling

These are not character defects. They are learned responses to danger.

---

## Slide 13 — The Rescue Boat
The Rescue Boat is what the person grabs when the survival moves are not enough. It kept them afloat — not free.
- Substances / Work / Control
- Isolation / Fantasy / Food
- Sex / Chaos / Another person

---

## Slide 14 — Why the Rescue Boat Matters
The Rescue Boat helped the person survive something they could not survive alone.  
But what once kept them alive may now be keeping them trapped. Understanding the boat is the first step toward replacing it.

---

## Slide 15 — The Way Back
The Way Back is what becomes possible when the whole pattern is visible. It is not a miracle or instant transformation.
- It is structure, repetition, and support.
- It is replacement and direction.

---

## Slide 16 — How the Way Back Works
One pause. One interrupt. One safer support. One repaired rupture. One moment where the old pattern does not get the final word.  
This is how direction begins.

---

## Slide 17 — From Map to Movement
The Survival Map explains the pattern.  
The Trajectory Engine explains the direction.  
Insight is not enough. Direction requires practice.

---

## Slide 18 — The Trajectory Engine
The Trajectory Engine is the mechanical relationship between:
**pattern → fork → interrupt → repetition → evidence → direction**

Trajectory is not fate — it is mechanics.

---

## Slide 19 — Why Trajectory Matters
A person can understand the pattern and still repeat it.  
The Trajectory Engine gives them leverage:  
one interrupt → repeated → until evidence forms → and direction becomes believable.

---

## Slide 20 — Why WRH Works
WRH is:
- non‑clinical / non‑punitive
- mechanical, not mystical
- repeatable / teachable / scalable

It gives people a map and a method.

---

## Slide 21 — What WRH Changes
WRH replaces:
- shame → sequence
- confusion → clarity
- pressure → possibility
- punishment → understanding
- hopelessness → direction

---

## Slide 22 — Best‑Fit Settings
WRH is designed for:
- reentry, veteran, and recovery programs
- peer‑support and diversion programs
- youth resilience, shelters, and rural outreach
- workforce‑readiness programs

---

## Slide 23 — Implementation Path
WRH can be implemented as:
- a full curriculum or modular training
- a staff‑training or participant‑facing program
- a trauma‑literacy foundation
- a decision‑making and accountability tool

---

## Slide 24 — Next Steps
1. Identify program goals
2. Select WRH modules
3. Train staff in the Survival Map + Trajectory Engine
4. Launch participant groups
5. Begin evidence tracking

Build direction, one repeatable move at a time.
WRH gives people a map of what happened — and a way to change what happens next.
