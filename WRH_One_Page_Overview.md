# WHAT REALLY HAPPENED (WRH)
A trauma‑literacy framework for understanding patterns, direction, and change.

## THE WRH PREMISE
People are not mysteries.
Patterns are not accidents.
Lives do not collapse out of nowhere.

WRH teaches one core truth:

> “What looks like failure is often survival.”

WRH gives participants a map of what happened, what their body learned, what pattern formed, and what becomes possible next.

## THE PROBLEM WRH SOLVES
Most systems judge people at the end of the pattern:
- the reaction
- the relapse
- the shutdown
- the explosion
- the avoidance
- the collapse

WRH asks a different question:
> “What happened before this happened?”

This shift turns judgment into understanding — and understanding into direction.

## THE SURVIVAL MAP
A five‑part framework for understanding where the pattern came from.

| Element | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **The Water** | The conditions a person was trying to survive |
| **The Alarm** | The body’s danger system |
| **The Survival Moves** | The automatic patterns that formed |
| **The Rescue Boat** | What kept the person afloat |
| **The Way Back** | The path that becomes possible when the pattern is visible |

The Survival Map gives people language for what they’ve only felt as confusion.

## THE TRAJECTORY ENGINE
A mechanical model for how direction changes.

Trajectory is built through:
- pattern
- fork
- interrupt
- repetition
- evidence
- direction

Trajectory is not fate. It is not destiny. It is not prophecy.

It is the path created when a pattern repeats — and the new path created when an interrupt repeats.

The Trajectory Engine turns insight into movement.

## WHY WRH WORKS
WRH is:
- non‑clinical
- non‑punitive
- mechanical, not mystical
- repeatable
- teachable
- scalable

WRH gives people:
- a map of their pattern
- a map of their choices
- a map of their consequences
- a map of their direction

It replaces shame with sequence. It replaces confusion with clarity. It replaces pressure with possibility.

## BEST‑FIT SETTINGS
WRH is designed for high‑barrier, high‑pressure environments:
- reentry programs
- veteran services
- recovery support
- peer‑support groups
- diversion programs
- court‑adjacent education
- youth resilience programs
- shelters
- rural outreach
- workforce‑readiness programs

WRH gives these systems a shared language for change.
